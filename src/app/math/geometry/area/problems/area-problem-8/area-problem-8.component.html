No matter the diameter of a circle, if you "unwrap" the circumference to make a straight line segment, it will be just over 3 times the length of the diameter. In fact, it will be times greater than the diameter. Pi is not a variable, it is a constant that always represents the same number, 3.14159...

If a circle has a diameter of 30, which value is closest to its circumference?

Note:
The diameter is the distance across a circle through its center.
The radius is the distance from the center of a circle to its circumference.
The circumference is the distance around a circle.

We know that the circumference is times greater than the diameter. Since the diameter is 30, the circumference will be 3.14 times greater.
